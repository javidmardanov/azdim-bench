Technical report · v0 ·

Frontier models sit Azerbaijan's national exams.

Every year the State Examination Center (DİM) writes thousands of expert-authored, high-stakes questions in a language most benchmarks ignore. We turned the official 2025–2026 exam archive into a contamination-aware benchmark — and graded fourteen language models with the same red pen.

A

Why Azerbaijani exams

§ 01 · Motivation

Azerbaijani is spoken by some thirty million people and is nearly invisible in LLM evaluation. Yet Azerbaijan runs one of the most centralized examination systems in the world: a single state body writes, administers, and — crucially — publishes the full text, explanations, and answer keys of its school-leaving and university entrance exams.

That gives us something rare: thousands of expert-written, curriculum-grounded, difficulty-calibrated questions with official gold answers, in parallel Azerbaijani and Russian sectors, refreshed on a public schedule — so items published after a model's training cutoff form a natural held-out set. High-stakes exams as a measurement instrument, not a scrape.

B

The dataset

§ 02 · Construction

Built from official DİM "izah" PDFs — the post-exam publications that reproduce each question with its choices, topic, grade level, worked explanation, and answer.

Extraction is the product. The PDFs' text layer silently corrupts mathematics (minus signs vanish; fractions collapse), so items were transcribed by a vision model under a strict JSON schema, then cross-checked against DİM's separate answer-key grids — parsed deterministically, with no LLM — and routed through human review.
12.5% → 100% GPT-5.4 mini on identical math items:
forced one-letter answers vs. allowed to reason first

Benchmarks that demand a bare answer letter measure obedience, not ability. Models that complied answered math blind and scored at chance; models that ignored the instruction reasoned out loud and aced the same questions. So our main condition follows the exam-hall rule instead: think as much as you like on scratch paper — the last line must be your answer.

C

Method

§ 03 · Protocol

Native-language prompts, visible reasoning, hidden reasoning minimized everywhere, answers parsed from a fixed final-line format. Same items, same rules, every model.
