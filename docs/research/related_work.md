# Related Work

This survey situates **AzDIM-Bench** — a benchmark of 2,249 items drawn from Azerbaijan's official state examinations administered by the State Examination Center (Dövlət İmtahan Mərkəzi, DİM; dim.gov.az), covering 11th-grade school-leaving (*buraxılış*) exams and the two-stage university-entrance exams (specialty groups I–IV), for the 2025 and 2026 cycles, across the Azerbaijani and Russian instructional sectors plus foreign-language sections (EN/DE/FR/AR). Items were extracted with a vision LLM from official *izah* (explanation) PDFs, cross-checked deterministically against DİM's separately published answer-key grids, and human-verified. We evaluate 14 LLMs under a reason-then-answer protocol with native-language prompts, simulate the official 700-point DİM score (including negative marking), and split 2025 vs. 2026 items to probe contamination.

We organize prior work into five strands: (1) national/regional exam benchmarks; (2) Azerbaijani and Turkic-language evaluation resources; (3) contamination-aware benchmark design; (4) exam-score simulation and human-anchored scoring; and (5) LLM-assisted dataset extraction and its validity concerns. A closing **Positioning** section states the novelty claims AzDIM-Bench can honestly make and flags the prior work that constrains each.

## 1. National and Regional Exam Benchmarks for LLMs

**AGIEval: A Human-Centric Benchmark for Evaluating Foundation Models.** Zhong, Cui, Guo, Liang, Lu, Wang, Saied, Chen, Duan. NAACL Findings 2024; arXiv:2304.06364. AGIEval draws from 20 official, high-stakes admission and qualification exams for humans — the Chinese Gaokao, the US SAT, LSAT, math competitions, lawyer-qualification and civil-service tests — evaluating models few-shot and few-shot-CoT, and anchoring results to average (50th-percentile) and top (1%) human performance. GPT-4 exceeded average human performance on SAT, LSAT and math competitions (95% on SAT Math). *AzDIM-Bench differs in being a single national system with a fully official machine-scoring pipeline (negative-marking-aware 700-point simulation) rather than a curated cross-national mixture reported as raw accuracy.*

**GAOKAO-Bench: Evaluating the Performance of Large Language Models on GAOKAO Benchmark.** Zhang, Li et al. arXiv:2305.12474 (2023). Compiles Chinese Gaokao questions from 2010–2022 including both objective (MCQ/fill-in) and subjective (free-response) items, evaluated zero-shot; it shows large per-subject disparities and that LLMs can grade subjective items with moderate human agreement. *AzDIM-Bench is entirely MCQ with deterministic scoring, avoiding the LLM-grading loop that GAOKAO-Bench needs for its subjective portion, and covers a different national curriculum.*

**GAOKAO-MM: A Chinese Human-Level Benchmark for Multimodal Models.** arXiv:2402.15745 (2024). A multimodal Gaokao subset requiring image understanding (diagrams, maps). *Relevant because AzDIM-Bench's source PDFs also contain figures; unlike GAOKAO-MM we currently evaluate the text-extractable MCQ subset rather than image-grounded reasoning.*

**C-Eval: A Multi-Level Multi-Discipline Chinese Evaluation Suite.** Huang et al. NeurIPS 2023 D&B; arXiv:2305.08322. 13,948 MCQs over 52 disciplines and four difficulty tiers (middle school → professional), plus a "C-Eval Hard" subset; only GPT-4 exceeded 60% average at release. *C-Eval is a broad curriculum-coverage suite; AzDIM-Bench instead mirrors one country's actual gatekeeping exams and reports the score a candidate would receive on the official scale.*

**KMMLU: Measuring Massive Multitask Language Understanding in Korean.** Son et al. arXiv:2402.11548 (2024). 35,030 expert-level MCQs over 45 subjects, collected from **original Korean exams rather than translated from English**, capturing native linguistic/cultural content; the best public model reached 50.5% vs. 62.6% human average. *AzDIM-Bench shares KMMLU's "native, not translated" philosophy but goes further by sourcing from a single official state-exam authority with published answer keys, enabling deterministic QC.*

**TurkishMMLU: Measuring Massive Multitask Language Understanding in Turkish.** Yüksel, Aydın et al. EMNLP Findings 2024; arXiv:2407.12402. First multitask Turkish MCQ benchmark: 10,000+ questions across 9 high-school subjects written by curriculum experts, with zero/few-shot, CoT and difficulty analyses over 20+ LLMs; it explicitly argues against automatic translation because it introduces cultural bias. *This is the closest Turkic-family analog by construction, but it covers Turkey's curriculum in Turkish; AzDIM-Bench covers Azerbaijan's DİM exams in Azerbaijani and Russian.*

**ArabicMMLU: Assessing Massive Multitask Language Understanding in Arabic.** Koto et al. ACL Findings 2024; arXiv:2402.12840. 14,575 native MSA MCQs across 40 tasks, sourced from school exams across North Africa, the Levant and the Gulf, built with native-speaker collaborators; a 100-sample QC pass reached 96% accuracy. *AzDIM-Bench parallels ArabicMMLU's native-exam sourcing and manual QC, but adds a deterministic answer-key cross-check rather than sample-based validation, and reports official exam scores.*

**IndoMMLU: Large Language Models Only Pass Primary School Exams in Indonesia.** Koto, Aji et al. EMNLP 2023; arXiv:2310.04928. 14,981 questions over 64 tasks from Indonesian primary-school through university-entrance exams, with 46% probing local languages/cultures; GPT-3.5 only passed the primary-school level. *AzDIM-Bench likewise spans school-leaving through university entrance for a single country, but focuses on the entrance-exam scoring formula as the headline metric.*

**Global-MMLU: Understanding and Addressing Cultural and Linguistic Biases in Multilingual Evaluation.** Singh et al. (Cohere For AI) arXiv:2412.03304 (2024). Improves MMLU across 42 languages using professional translation + crowd post-edits, and annotates cultural sensitivity (2,850 items/language); it finds 28% of MMLU items need culturally sensitive knowledge and 84.9% of geographic items are North-American/European. *Global-MMLU is fundamentally a translated/curated MMLU; AzDIM-Bench is natively authored by a state examination body, so cultural grounding is intrinsic rather than annotated post hoc. Global-MMLU does not include Azerbaijani exam content.*

**MMLU-ProX: A Multilingual Benchmark for Advanced LLM Evaluation.** Xuan, Weihao et al. EMNLP 2025; arXiv:2503.10497. Extends the harder MMLU-Pro to 13 (later 29) typologically diverse languages via LLM translation + expert verification, ~11,829 parallel items/language, revealing up to 24.3% high-to-low-resource performance gaps. *A parallel-translation design; AzDIM-Bench is non-parallel, native and exam-authentic, and the AZ/RU pairing arises from the actual dual-sector exam rather than translation.*

**EXAMS: A Multi-Subject High School Examinations Dataset.** Hardalov, Mihaylov, Zlatkova, Dinkov, Koychev, Nakov. EMNLP 2020; arXiv:2011.03080. 24,000+ high-school exam MCQs in **16 languages** (Albanian, Arabic, Bulgarian, Croatian, French, German, Hungarian, Italian, Lithuanian, Macedonian, Polish, Portuguese, Serbian, Spanish, Turkish, Vietnamese) across 24 subjects, collected from ministries of education, for cross-lingual/multilingual QA. **Turkish is the only Turkic language; Azerbaijani is NOT included.** *This is the canonical multilingual state-exam benchmark; crucially it contains no Azerbaijani items, which underpins our "first" claim.*

**EXAMS-V: A Multi-Discipline Multilingual Multimodal Exam Benchmark for VLMs.** Das, Hristov, Li, Dimitrov, Koychev, Nakov. ACL 2024; arXiv:2403.10378. 20,932 MCQs over 20 disciplines in **11 languages** (English, Chinese, French, German, Italian, Arabic, Polish, Hungarian, Bulgarian, Croatian, Serbian) from 7 families, with rich multimodal features; PDFs were converted to cropped images and **manually** bounding-boxed by the authors. **Azerbaijani is NOT among the 11 languages.** *EXAMS-V is the nearest methodological cousin (state-exam PDFs → structured items), but it used manual annotation and excludes Azerbaijani; AzDIM-Bench automates extraction via a vision LLM with deterministic answer-key verification and targets Azerbaijan.*

**M3Exam: A Multilingual, Multimodal, Multilevel Benchmark.** Zhang, Aljunied, Gao, Chia, Bing. NeurIPS 2023 D&B; arXiv:2306.05179. 12,317 questions from real official exams in 9 languages (incl. English, Chinese, Italian, Portuguese, Vietnamese, Thai, Javanese, Swahili, Afrikaans) at three educational levels, ~23% requiring images; GPT-4 still struggled on low-resource, non-Latin scripts. **Azerbaijani is not covered.** *M3Exam's multilevel/official-exam design is close in spirit; AzDIM-Bench adds a single-country deep dive with official score simulation and a contamination split.*

**Kaleidoscope: In-language Exams for Massively Multilingual Vision Evaluation.** Salazar et al. arXiv:2504.07072 (2025). A large open-science VLM benchmark: 20,911 in-language MCQs across 18 languages and 14 subjects, curated by a worldwide researcher collaboration to preserve cultural authenticity; top VLMs underperform on low-resource languages. *Kaleidoscope emphasizes in-language authenticity (as we do) but is breadth-first across many countries; it does not include Azerbaijani DİM exams, and does not simulate national score scales.*

**2026 successors / adjacent recent work.** SinhalaMMLU (arXiv:2509.03162), VMMU for Vietnamese (arXiv:2508.13680), BhashaBench for Indic domains (arXiv:2510.25409) and the Korean CSAT study below all continue the "native national exam → LLM benchmark" trajectory into 2025–2026, none covering Azerbaijani. *AzDIM-Bench fills the remaining Azerbaijani/DİM gap in this rapidly growing family.*

## 2. Azerbaijani and Turkic-Language Evaluation Resources

**Open Foundation Models for the Azerbaijani Language.** Isbarov, Huseynova, Mammadov, Hajili, Ataman. arXiv:2407.02337 (2024). Introduces the DOLLMA corpus (651.1M words), a family of encoder-only Azerbaijani models, and evaluation datasets: AZE-SCI (text classification), AZE-NSP (next-sentence prediction) and CB-MCQ (closed-book QA); it benchmarks major open models with Azerbaijani support. *This is the foundational open-Azerbaijani-NLP paper, but its evaluation sets are constructed NLU tasks, not official national exams, and it targets encoder models rather than reasoning-capable generative LLMs on gatekeeping exams.*

**TUMLU: A Unified and Native Language Understanding Benchmark for Turkic Languages.** Isbarov, Akhundjanova, Hajili, Huseynova, Gaynullin, Rzayev, Tursun, Turdubaeva, Saetov, Kharisov, Belginova, Kenbayeva, Alisheva, Köksal, Rustamov, Ataman. ACL 2025 (Main); arXiv:2502.11020. A native middle/high-school MCQ benchmark spanning 8 Turkic languages (Azerbaijani, Crimean Tatar, Karakalpak, Kazakh, Kyrgyz, Tatar, Turkish, Uyghur, Uzbek) over 11 subjects (38,139 items total), plus a balanced, manually verified **TUMLU-mini** subset. **The Azerbaijani split (~100 items/subject over 7 subjects, i.e. ~700 items) was gathered from publicly available books and websites and community-developed; the authors report ~10% of the Azerbaijani questions were invalid or had incorrect answers before manual cleaning.** *This is the single most important prior work for our Azerbaijani claims: TUMLU **does** include Azerbaijani school-subject MCQs. However, its Azerbaijani items come from textbooks/websites, not from DİM's official state-exam PDFs, have no official answer key to verify against (hence the ~10% error rate), carry no official scoring scale, and lack a Russian-sector parallel or a 2025/2026 contamination split. AzDIM-Bench is therefore the first Azerbaijani benchmark built from official DİM state-exam items with deterministic answer-key QC and official score simulation.*

**Cetvel: A Unified Benchmark for Evaluating Language Understanding, Generation and Cultural Capacity of LLMs for Turkish.** Aydın et al. (KUIS-AI). EACL 2026; arXiv:2508.16431. 23 tasks in 7 categories (discriminative + generative, incl. Turkish history/idioms), evaluating 33 open-weight LLMs; Turkish-centric instruction-tuned models often underperform general multilingual ones. **TurkBench** (arXiv:2601.07020) is a further 2026 Turkish benchmark. *These deepen Turkish (not Azerbaijani) evaluation and are generative/holistic rather than exam-score-anchored.*

**Azerbaijani LLM Leaderboard** (LLM-Beetle, Hugging Face Space). A community leaderboard aggregating Azerbaijani LLM evaluations. *A useful venue and baseline surface, but not a peer-reviewed, exam-grounded benchmark with official scoring; AzDIM-Bench could be contributed to such a leaderboard.*

The broader Turkic/low-resource literature (e.g. Luxembourgish proficiency-exam probing, arXiv:2504.01667; multilingual education-bias analyses, arXiv:2504.17720) confirms that low-resource, morphologically rich languages remain systematically underserved — the gap AzDIM-Bench addresses for Azerbaijani specifically.

## 3. Contamination-Aware Benchmark Design

**MMLU-CF: A Contamination-free Multi-task Language Understanding Benchmark.** Zhao et al. (Microsoft). ACL 2025; arXiv:2412.15194. Rebuilds MMLU-style evaluation to resist leakage: sources from a broad domain, applies three decontamination rules, and splits into an open validation set and a **closed test set**; GPT-4o reaches only ~73% 5-shot. *AzDIM-Bench addresses contamination differently — via a temporal 2025-vs-2026 split where 2026 items post-date most training cutoffs — while keeping items human-inspectable rather than closed-source.*

**LiveBench: A Challenging, Contamination-Free (Contamination-Limited) LLM Benchmark.** White, Dooley et al. arXiv:2406.19314 (2024). Frequently refreshed questions from recent sources (new math competitions, arXiv papers, news) with objective automatic scoring across six categories; ~1/6 of items are replaced monthly so the benchmark fully refreshes roughly every six months; top models score below 65%. *AzDIM-Bench is naturally "live" because DİM releases new official exams each cycle; the 2025→2026 rollover is our built-in temporal refresh, tied to a real high-stakes exam rather than aggregated web sources.*

**Benchmarking LLMs Under Data Contamination: From Static to Dynamic Evaluation** (survey). arXiv:2502.17521 (2025), and **A Comprehensive Survey of Contamination Detection Methods** (arXiv:2404.00699). These synthesize the temporal-cutoff strategy (LiveBench, LiveCodeBench, LiveAoPS-Bench) and its known limitation: post-cutoff items can still leak if competition problems are reused, so a post-cutoff split is necessary but not sufficient. *AzDIM-Bench inherits this caveat — DİM items are official and freshly released, reducing reuse risk relative to open competition pools, but we frame the 2026 split as a contamination probe rather than a guarantee.*

**Test of Time: Rethinking Temporal Signal of Benchmark Contamination.** arXiv:2509.00072 (2025). Argues that post-cutoff performance decay is itself a sensitive contamination signal, motivating explicit before/after comparisons. *This directly motivates AzDIM-Bench's core design decision: reporting 2025 vs. 2026 deltas per model as a contamination diagnostic rather than a single aggregate number.*

## 4. Exam-Score Simulation and Human-Anchored Scoring

**GPT-4 Technical Report and standardized-exam claims** (OpenAI, 2023). GPT-4 was reported at ~90th percentile on a uniform bar exam, 88th on the LSAT, 93rd on SAT EBRW, plus GRE percentiles — reporting human-scale percentiles rather than raw accuracy. **Re-Evaluating GPT-4's Bar Exam Performance** (Martínez, Institute for Law & AI, 2023) showed the headline bar percentile was inflated (closer to ~62nd–69th percentile against first-time takers), a cautionary tale about score-scale reporting. *AzDIM-Bench reports scores on the exact official DİM 700-point scale with the published conversion, avoiding unofficial percentile estimation, and we surface the negative-marking correction that percentile claims typically omit.*

**Evaluating LLMs on the 2026 Korean CSAT Mathematics Exam in a Zero-Data-Leakage Setting.** *Applied Artificial Intelligence* / arXiv:2511.18649 (2026). All 46 CSAT math items were digitized within two hours of release to guarantee no training-data inclusion, then 24 LLMs were scored across modalities/prompt languages; GPT-5-family models reached perfect scores under some configs. *This is the strongest recent analog for AzDIM-Bench's twin design goals — national-exam scoring plus contamination control — but it is math-only and single-year; AzDIM-Bench spans full multi-subject exams, two sectors, and an explicit two-year split.*

**SciEx: Benchmarking LLMs on Scientific Exams with Human and Automatic Grading.** arXiv:2406.10421 (2024). University science exams graded by both human experts and LLM-judges, quantifying grading agreement. *Reinforces the value of human-anchored scoring; AzDIM-Bench sidesteps judge subjectivity because DİM MCQs have deterministic keys, so our "grading" is the official answer key, not an LLM.*

**Human-ability / meta-study framing** (e.g. "Can the capability of LLMs be described by human ability? A meta study," arXiv:2504.12332, 2025) and **Reference-Anchored Elo Estimation** (RAEE, OpenReview 2025) formalize anchoring model scores to human or reference baselines. *AzDIM-Bench's anchor is the operational cut-scores and 700-point curve that actually determine Azerbaijani university admission — a concrete, policy-relevant anchor rather than an abstract Elo.*

**Abstention and negative marking.** **AbstentionBench** (Meta; arXiv:2506.09038, NeurIPS 2025) shows frontier LLMs largely fail to abstain even when they should, and that scale barely helps. *This is directly relevant: DİM's negative-marking formula rewards calibrated abstention (leaving an item blank beats guessing wrong), so AzDIM-Bench's score simulation implicitly measures a capability AbstentionBench isolates — but under a real high-stakes penalty structure rather than a synthetic one.*

## 5. LLM-Assisted Dataset Extraction and Validity Concerns

**Silencer: From Discovery to Mitigation of Self-Bias in LLM-as-Benchmark-Generator.** Zhang et al. NeurIPS 2025; arXiv:2505.20738. Defines and validates *self-bias* — models score inflated on benchmarks they themselves generated — decomposing it into question-domain, language-style and wrong-label sub-biases, and proposes a multi-generator framework that suppresses self-bias to near zero (Pearson correlation with human-annotated benchmarks rising 0.655→0.833). *This is the central circularity critique AzDIM-Bench must answer: we do NOT let the LLM invent items or labels. The vision LLM only transcribes pre-existing human-authored questions and official answers; ground-truth labels come from DİM's independent answer-key grids, breaking the wrong-label and question-domain self-bias channels Silencer identifies.*

**Deconstructing Self-Bias in LLM-Generated (Translation) Benchmarks.** Xu et al. arXiv:2509.26600 (2025). Shows self-bias has two additive sources — the generator LLM and the judge LLM — and that GPT models identify their own generations with 98–99% accuracy, far above embedding models. **Do LLM Evaluators Prefer Themselves for a Reason?** (arXiv:2504.03846, 2025) probes whether self-preference tracks genuine quality. *AzDIM-Bench neutralizes the judge channel entirely (deterministic key-based scoring, no LLM judge) and constrains the generator channel to faithful transcription, so the only residual risk is extraction fidelity, which we control by cross-checking against the official key.*

**OmniDocBench: Benchmarking Diverse PDF Document Parsing.** Ouyang et al. arXiv:2412.07626 (2024), plus VLM-vs-OCR PDF-extraction studies (e.g. French PDF-to-Markdown, arXiv:2602.11960) and multilingual OCR-pipeline reports (Surya/Tesseract/Google Vision comparisons in BhashaBench, arXiv:2510.25409). These quantify where VLM/OCR extraction fails — dense layouts, tables, figures, non-Latin scripts — and motivate explicit extraction QC. *AzDIM-Bench's deterministic answer-key cross-check is precisely such a QC gate: any item whose extracted answer disagrees with DİM's published key is flagged for human review, giving a per-item correctness signal that pure OCR-similarity metrics cannot.*

**Circularity mitigation as a design principle.** The combined message of Silencer, Xu et al. and the self-preference literature is that using an LLM anywhere in a benchmark that evaluates LLMs demands explicit mitigations. *AzDIM-Bench's three-layer defense — (i) LLM used only for transcription of human-authored items, never generation; (ii) deterministic verification against an independent official key; (iii) human verification of flagged items — is a concrete instantiation of the mitigations these papers call for, and should be argued as such in the paper.*

## Positioning

Against this literature, AzDIM-Bench's strongest, defensible novelty claims are:

1. **First LLM benchmark built from Azerbaijan's official DİM state exams.** No prior state-exam benchmark covers Azerbaijani: **EXAMS** (16 languages) and **EXAMS-V** (11 languages) both include Turkish but *not* Azerbaijani; **M3Exam**, **Kaleidoscope**, **Global-MMLU** and **MMLU-ProX** likewise omit it. **This claim must be stated precisely as "first from official DİM/national exams," because TUMLU (ACL 2025) already contains an Azerbaijani school-subject MCQ split.** The honest distinction is *provenance and QC*: TUMLU's Azerbaijani items come from textbooks/websites (community-sourced, no official key, ~10% pre-cleaning error rate), whereas AzDIM-Bench's come from DİM's official *izah* PDFs with the official answer key. Do not claim "first Azerbaijani benchmark" unqualified.

2. **First benchmark (in any language, to our knowledge) with fully deterministic official-answer-key cross-verification of every item.** ArabicMMLU and TUMLU-mini use *sample-based* or manual QC; EXAMS-V used manual bounding-box annotation. AzDIM-Bench uniquely verifies every extracted answer against DİM's independently published answer-key grid, converting extraction QC from a spot-check into a per-item guarantee — a direct, concrete mitigation of the LLM-extraction circularity risk that Silencer and the self-bias literature warn about.

3. **First to simulate the official national score scale, including negative marking.** AGIEval and the GPT-4 exam claims report percentiles (sometimes inflated); C-Eval/KMMLU/TurkishMMLU report accuracy; the Korean CSAT study scores raw exam points but is math-only and single-year. AzDIM-Bench implements DİM's actual 700-point conversion *with* the negative-marking correction, making it (to our knowledge) the first multi-subject national-exam LLM benchmark whose headline metric is the operational admission score, and one of very few to make guess-vs-abstain penalties first-class (connecting to AbstentionBench).

4. **Parallel Azerbaijani/Russian instructional sectors plus a native 2025-vs-2026 contamination split.** The AZ/RU pairing is *native and non-translated* (the same exam is administered in both sectors), distinguishing it from translation-based parallel corpora (Global-MMLU, MMLU-ProX). The temporal split operationalizes the post-cutoff contamination probe advocated by "Test of Time" and the contamination surveys, using freshly released official exams (lower reuse risk than open competition pools à la LiveBench).

**Claims that prior work weakens or forbids:**
- **"First Azerbaijani LLM benchmark"** — forbidden. TUMLU (2502.11020) and the Open-Foundation-Models paper (2407.02337) precede us with Azerbaijani evaluation sets; scope the claim to *official state exams / DİM provenance*.
- **"First multilingual state-exam benchmark"** — forbidden. EXAMS/EXAMS-V/M3Exam/Kaleidoscope hold that ground; our contribution is the Azerbaijani/DİM coverage they lack.
- **"First to use vision-LLM PDF extraction for an exam benchmark"** — weak. EXAMS-V already went PDF→structured items (manually), and VLM PDF extraction is now common (OmniDocBench and successors); our novelty is the *deterministic official-key verification layer*, not the extraction itself.
- **"First contamination-aware exam benchmark"** — weak. LiveBench, MMLU-CF and the 2026 Korean CSAT study precede us; our contribution is applying a *native post-cutoff split to a full national multi-subject exam in a low-resource language*.

In short, AzDIM-Bench's honest position is: **the first official-DİM-provenance, answer-key-verified, official-score-simulating (negative-marking-aware) benchmark for Azerbaijan's national exams, with parallel AZ/RU sectors and a 2025/2026 contamination split** — building directly on TUMLU and the Azerbaijani-NLP line for language coverage, on EXAMS-V/M3Exam for exam-PDF methodology, on KMMLU/TurkishMMLU/ArabicMMLU for the native-not-translated principle, and on Silencer/LiveBench/Test-of-Time for its circularity and contamination mitigations.
